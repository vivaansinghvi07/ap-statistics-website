Bias
  • Bias in a study is when it consistently either overestimates or underestimates a value that it is trying to determine
    • There are several types of bias
  • Nonresponse bias is when some of the selected individuals (through sampling) do not response to or refuse to participate in the study
    • The individuals that do respond may hold more passionate views on the topic, messing with the data
  • Undercoverage bias is when the sapling method is unable to reach a certain group of individuals
    • For example, if you are sampling by calling landline phones, the households without landlines will be left out the survey entirely
  • Response bias is when the respondent of a survey answers a question in an unintended manner, for whatever reason
    • It could be due to confusing wording of the question itself 
    • It could also be due to the respondent's own personal feelings (i.e. embarrassment)
